Heritage

60 Years of History

From a sketch on a napkin to the most recognisable sports car on the planet.

1963
The Birth — Frankfurt Motor Show
Ferdinand "Butzi" Porsche unveils the 901 (later 911) to the world. A rear-engine, air-cooled flat-six defines the silhouette that will never be forgotten.
1973
Carrera RS 2.7 — The Holy Grail
Lightweight, ducktail spoiler, 210 hp. The RS 2.7 becomes the most coveted 911 ever built. Only 1,580 made. Now worth millions.
1989
964 — The Modern Era Begins
Major redesign brings ABS, power steering and all-wheel drive Carrera 4. Still unmistakably 911, yet refined for a new generation.
1997
996 — Water-Cooled Revolution
After 34 years, Porsche switches to water-cooling. Controversial at the time, it allows higher performance and stricter emissions compliance.
2011
991 — The Digital Age
Longer wheelbase, electric steering, and the first 911 with a 7-speed manual. Sets the template for the modern era of the 911.
2019
992 — Pinnacle of Perfection
Widebody design, wet mode, night vision assist. The 992 is the most technologically advanced 911 ever, while staying true to its soul.
